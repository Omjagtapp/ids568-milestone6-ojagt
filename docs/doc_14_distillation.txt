Title: Knowledge Distillation

Knowledge distillation trains a smaller student model to mimic the output distribution of a larger teacher model. The student minimises a combination of the cross-entropy loss on ground-truth labels and a KL-divergence loss against the teacher's soft probabilities (at a temperature T). DistilBERT achieves 97% of BERT performance at 60% of its size and 40% fewer parameters. Task-specific distillation (task-KD) distils on a labelled dataset while task-agnostic distillation transfers general language understanding. Distillation is distinct from quantisation, which reduces numerical precision of existing weights.
